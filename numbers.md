# Numbers (数字 - Sūji)

Japanese numbers follow a very logical pattern. Here are the basics for JLPT N5.

## Basic Numbers 1-10

| Number | Kanji | Hiragana | Romaji |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | 零 / 〇 | れい / ぜろ | rei / zero |
| 1 | 一 | いち | ichi |
| 2 | 二 | に | ni |
| 3 | 三 | さん | san |
| 4 | 四 | し / よん | shi / yon |
| 5 | 五 | ご | go |
| 6 | 六 | ろく | roku |
| 7 | 七 | しち / なな | shichi / nana |
| 8 | 八 | はち | hachi |
| 9 | 九 | きゅう / く | kyū / ku |
| 10 | 十 | じゅう | jū |

## Numbers 11-99

Forming numbers 11-99 is simple:
- **11-19**: 10 + [digit] (e.g., 11 is 十一 - jū-ichi)
- **20, 30, etc.**: [digit] + 10 (e.g., 20 is 二十 - ni-jū)
- **21-99**: [digit] + 10 + [digit] (e.g., 25 is 二十五 - ni-jū-go)

## Large Numbers

| Value | Kanji | Hiragana | Romaji |
|---|---|---|---|
| 100 | 百 | ひゃく | hyaku |
| 1,000 | 千 | せん | sen |
| 10,000 | 万 | まん | man |

> [!NOTE]
> - **Silent Zero**: If 0 is in the middle of a number, it's silent. For example, **409** is **四百九** (yon-hyaku-kyū).
> - **Irregular Readings**: Pay attention to irregular readings like 300 (san-byaku), 600 (rop-pyaku), 800 (hap-pyaku), 3,000 (san-zen), and 8,000 (has-sen).
> - **4-Digit Units (Myriad system)**: Unlike Western systems that use 3-digit units (thousands), Japanese uses **4-digit units** based on **万 (man - 10,000)**.
>   - *Example 1*: **12,000** is thought of as **1,2000** → **一万二千** (*ichi-man ni-sen*).
>   - *Example 2*: **100,000** is thought of as **10,0000** → **10万** (*jū-man*).
>   - *Example 3*: **6,023,080** is thought of as **602,3080** → **六百二万三千八十** (*rop-pyaku ni-man san-zen hachi-jū*).
